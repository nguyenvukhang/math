title: "Theorem 1.1.1 (Fundamental theorem of calculus)"
open: true
---

### First part

Let $f:[a,b]\to\R$. Let $F:[a,b]\to\R$ be defined by

$$
F(x)=\int_a^xf(t)\,dt
$$

Then $F$ is uniformly continuous on $[a,b]$ and differentiable on
$(a,b)$, and

$$
F'(x)=f(x)
$$

on $(a,b)$ so $F$ is an antiderivative of $f$.
